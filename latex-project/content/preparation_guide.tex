\section{Probability \& Statistics}
\label{sec:prep-probability}

This section covers both the official syllabus and advanced topics from past exams. Items marked \textit{(advanced)} go beyond undergraduate-level probability.

\subsection{Foundations of Probability}

Sample space, events, classical probability, geometric probability, probability axioms, conditional probability, Bayes' theorem, independence. Borel--Cantelli lemma.

Convergence theorems: convergence in probability, in distribution, in $L^p$, almost sure convergence, SLLN, CLT, law of iterated logarithm (LIL), Berry--Esseen. Concentration inequalities: Hoeffding, Khinchin, Rademacher--Menshov.

\subsection{Random Variables and Distributions}

Discrete: binomial, Poisson, hypergeometric, negative binomial, geometric. Continuous: uniform, exponential, normal (Gaussian), gamma, beta, Dirichlet. Distribution functions, pmf/pdf, transformation of random variables.

Multivariate distributions; joint and marginal; conditional distributions; convolution. Covariance, correlation, independence. MGF, CF, moments. Order statistics; records; exponential minima. Stein's lemma. Multivariate normal; chi-squared, $t$, $F$ distributions.

\subsection{Mathematical Expectation}

Expectation, variance, standard deviation. Conditional expectation; tower property. Wald's equation; recursive equation; generating function method. Martingale; optional stopping; martingale convergence.

Random walk; gambler's ruin; hitting probabilities. Queueing theory (M/M/1, birth--death). Renewal theory; overlapping patterns. Coupling; total variation distance. Foster--Lyapunov drift criterion.

\subsection{Stochastic Processes}

Markov chains: state space, transition matrix, Chapman--Kolmogorov, recurrent/transient classification, stationary distribution, limit distribution, reversibility, ergodicity. Branching process; extinction probability.

Poisson process: construction, interarrival times, superposition, thinning. Brownian motion (Wiener process): martingale properties, hitting times, reflection principle.

Urn models: Polya's urn, reinforcement. Random graphs: Erd\H{o}s--R\'enyi $G(n,p)$, first and second moment method, phase transition, connectivity threshold, largest clique.

\subsection{Statistical Estimation and Decision Theory}

MLE; score function; Fisher information; asymptotic efficiency. UMVUE; Cram\'er--Rao lower bound. James--Stein shrinkage; admissibility. Decision theory: loss, risk, Bayes risk, minimax risk; optimal testing (Neyman--Pearson); confidence intervals; minimax intervals.

GLM: link function, logistic regression, Poisson regression. Lasso; $L^1$ regularization; KKT conditions.

Kernel density estimation (Epanechnikov). Nonparametric tests: Wilcoxon signed-rank, Wilcoxon--Mann--Whitney, permutation tests, Fisher exact test.

\subsection{Causal Inference and Experimental Design}

Neyman--Rubin causal model; potential outcomes; SUTVA; ATT vs ATE. Fisher exact test for $H_0$. Randomized experiment; randomization inference; re-randomization. IPW; propensity score; covariate adjustment; ANCOVA.

\section{Applied \& Computational Mathematics}
\label{sec:prep-applied}

This section covers both the official syllabus and computationally-focused topics from past exams. Items marked \textit{(advanced)} reflect verified exam content.

\subsection{Calculus (syllabus)}

Functions, limits, continuity. Derivatives: tangent lines, optimization, related rates. Integrals: indefinite, definite, improper; FTC; techniques (substitution, parts, partial fractions, trig substitution).

Sequences and series; convergence tests. Power series; Taylor series. Multivariable: partial derivatives, gradient, divergence, curl, Jacobian. Multiple integrals. Line and surface integrals. Green, Gauss, Stokes theorems.

ODEs: separable, linear, exact, integrating factor. Higher-order linear ODEs with constant coefficients. Systems of first-order ODEs; phase portraits. PDEs: classification (elliptic, parabolic, hyperbolic); canonical equations (heat, wave, Laplace).

\subsection{Linear Algebra (syllabus)}

Determinants; cofactor expansion. Matrix operations; inverse matrices; Sherman--Morrison rank-one update. Gaussian elimination; LU; RREF. Vector spaces: subspaces, basis, dimension, coordinates. Linear independence; rank--nullity.

Eigenvalues and eigenvectors; characteristic polynomial; Cayley--Hamilton. Spectral theorem for symmetric matrices. Inner product; Gram--Schmidt. QR decomposition; least squares. Quadratic forms. Gershgorin disc theorem.

\subsection{Probability (syllabus)}

Sample spaces, events, probability axioms; conditional probability; Bayes. Random variables: discrete and continuous distributions. Joint distributions; marginal and conditional. Expectation, variance, covariance, correlation. MGF and CF. LLN; CLT. Conditional expectation.

\subsection{Operations Research (syllabus)}

LP: formulation, geometry, simplex, two-phase, big-M, duality. Sensitivity analysis. Integer LP; branch-and-bound.

Transportation and assignment; Hungarian algorithm. Network flows: Dijkstra, Floyd--Warshall, Kruskal, Prim, Ford--Fulkerson. CPM/PERT. Decision analysis: decision trees, expected value, risk. Game theory: zero-sum, Nash equilibrium, mixed strategies.

\subsection{Finite Difference Methods for PDEs} \textit{(advanced)}

Consistency, stability, convergence; Lax equivalence theorem. Von Neumann stability analysis; CFL condition.

Explicit schemes: FTCS for heat equation; stability constraints. Upwind scheme; Beam--Warming; Lax--Wendroff. Leapfrog scheme.

Implicit schemes: BTCS; Crank--Nicolson ($\theta$-scheme); unconditional stability. Richardson extrapolation. Finite difference for wave equation and Laplace/Poisson.

\subsection{Finite Element Methods (FEM)} \textit{(advanced)}

Weak (variational) formulation of BVPs; Ritz--Galerkin. CG and DG methods; piecewise polynomial spaces ($P_1$, $P_2$). A priori error estimates; convergence rates in $H^1$ and $L^2$. Superconvergence at Gaussian points. FEM for elliptic BVPs: Poisson equation.

\subsection{Numerical Linear Algebra} \textit{(advanced)}

SVD; low-rank approximation via SVD; Eckart--Young. QR: Gram--Schmidt (classical and modified), Householder, Givens. Vandermonde least-squares approximation.

Eigenvalue algorithms: power method; inverse iteration; shifted power method; QR algorithm. Arnoldi process; Krylov subspaces; Lanczos. CG; GMRES; Richardson; SOR; Jacobi; Gauss--Seidel. Spectral radius of tridiagonal (Toeplitz) matrices; Gershgorin discs.

\subsection{Numerical Methods for ODEs} \textit{(advanced)}

Forward Euler, backward Euler, improved Euler, RK4. Consistency, order, convergence; absolute stability regions. BDF; BDF2 zero-stability and order. SSP-RK; IMEX RK. Multistep: Adams--Bashforth, Adams--Moulton.

Stiff ODEs; A-stability, L-stability. Banach fixed-point for ODE convergence.

Secant method (superlinear convergence); Steffensen acceleration; bisection; Newton's method.

\subsection{Optimization and Convex Analysis} \textit{(advanced)}

KKT conditions; complementary slackness; dual feasibility. Lagrangian duality; weak and strong duality; Slater's condition. Lasso regression; $L^1$ regularization; compressed sensing. Convex sets and functions; co-coercivity; proximal operators; FBP; Douglas--Rachford.

Subgradient and subdifferential calculus; Frank--Wolfe basics.

\subsection{Calculus of Variations and Euler--Lagrange} \textit{(advanced)}

Euler--Lagrange equation; necessary and sufficient conditions; natural boundary conditions. Isoperimetric problems; isoperimetric inequality. Legendre condition; Jacobi conjugate points. Noether's theorem. Direct method; Ritz method.

\subsection{Orthogonal Polynomials and Spectral Methods} \textit{(advanced)}

Legendre polynomials: orthogonality, Rodrigues formula, recurrence. Chebyshev ($T_n$, $U_n$): orthogonality with weight $(1-x^2)^{-1/2}$; minimax property.

Gaussian quadrature: Gauss--Legendre, Gauss--Chebyshev; error estimates. Clenshaw--Curtis; Lobatto; Radau.

Chebyshev interpolation; spectral collocation. Moving least squares (MLS). Fourier spectral methods; spectral accuracy. Crank--Nicolson spectral for diffusion.

\subsection{Dynamical Systems and Biological Modeling} \textit{(advanced)}

Phase plane; fixed points; linearization via Jacobian; stability; Lyapunov stability. Bifurcations: saddle-node, transcritical, pitchfork, Hopf.

FitzHugh--Nagumo neuronal model; excitability. Michaelis--Menten enzyme kinetics; steady-state approximation; Lineweaver--Burk plot.

Singular perturbation; boundary layers; matched asymptotic expansions. WKB for Schr\"odinger equations. Hamilton--Jacobi equations; shock formation; Rankine--Hugoniot conditions.

\subsection{Graph Theory (Advanced)}

Eulerian and Hamiltonian graphs; Dirac's theorem ($n\geq3$, $\deg(v)\geq n/2$); Ore's theorem. Bondy--Chvatal closure. Hall's marriage theorem; Tutte's theorem. Planar graphs; Kuratowski ($K_5$, $K_{3,3}$). Graph coloring; chromatic polynomial.

\subsection{Number Theory}

Divisibility; Euclidean algorithm; prime numbers. Congruences; Chinese remainder theorem. Fermat's little theorem; Euler's theorem; $\varphi(n)$. RSA encryption. Primitive roots; discrete logarithms. Quadratic residues; Legendre and Jacobi symbols. Eisenstein's criterion.

\subsection{Learning Theory and VC Dimension} \textit{(advanced)}

PAC learning framework; sample complexity. VC dimension: definition, growth function, Sauer's lemma. VC dimension of interval classifiers in $\mathbb{R}$ equals 2. Rademacher complexity basics.
